Your plan gives you a set number of generations each month. Understanding what counts as one helps you get the most from your plan.

## What counts as one generation

- **One article personalization = one generation.** Personalizing a single PLR article or draft uses one generation.
- **One social post request = one generation.** A social post request uses one generation no matter how many platforms it covers. Generating posts for all six platforms at once still counts as one.

<Tip>
  Because a full six-platform social set is a single generation, generating your article and its matching social posts together is an efficient way to use your pool.
</Tip>

## The pool is shared

Articles and social posts draw from the same monthly pool. There is no separate allowance for each. You decide how to split your generations between long-form personalization and social content.

## Monthly limits by plan

| Plan | Generations / mo |
| --- | --- |
| Free | 6 (grandfathered free accounts: 10) |
| Basic | 50 |
| Premium | 150 |

## When the pool resets

When your pool resets depends on your plan:

- **Free (including grandfathered free accounts):** resets on the 1st of each calendar month (UTC).
- **Basic and Premium:** reset on your monthly billing anniversary — the same day of the month you first subscribed. For example, if you started your subscription on the 14th, your pool refreshes on the 14th of every month.

Annual subscribers still get a monthly allowance that refreshes on the same day-of-month anchor, not a single yearly bucket. If your anniversary day does not exist in a shorter month (for example, the 31st in February), the reset falls on the last day of that month and returns to your usual day the following month.

The usage banner in the app always shows your personal reset date, such as **"Your allowance resets Aug 14"**, so you don't have to calculate it yourself. Changing plans mid-cycle does not hand you a fresh allowance — the anniversary day is preserved.

If you need more than your plan allows, you can upgrade at any time. Before you do, read [Beta & Grandfathered Pricing](/plans/beta-and-grandfathering), because changing tiers affects any locked-in beta rate.
