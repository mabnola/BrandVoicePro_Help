Your plan gives you a set number of generations each month. Understanding what counts as one helps you get the most from your plan.

## What counts as one generation

- **One article personalization = one generation.** Personalizing a single PLR article or draft on the Customize tab uses one generation.
- **One social post request = one generation.** A request on the Collateral tab uses one generation no matter how many platforms it covers. All six platforms at once still counts as one.

What does not count:

- **SEO title options.** The five titles generated after each article personalization are free.
- **Creating or editing Brand Profiles.** Free on every plan.
- **A request blocked by the word limit.** If your source text is over your plan's limit, the request is stopped before it starts and nothing is deducted.

<Tip>
  Because a full six-platform social set is a single generation, select every platform you publish on before generating rather than running one platform at a time.
</Tip>

## The pool is shared

Articles and social posts draw from the same monthly pool. There is no separate allowance for each. You decide how to split your generations between long-form personalization and social content.

Your current usage is shown in the app, and the counter updates as soon as a generation finishes.

<Note>
  A generation is counted when the request starts, not when it finishes. If you stop a generation partway through, it still counts.
</Note>

## Monthly limits by plan

| Plan | Generations / mo | Words per generation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Free | 6 (grandfathered free accounts: 10) | 1,500 |
| Basic | 50 | 3,000 |
| Premium | 150 | 5,000 |

The word limit applies to the content you paste in, not to the content that comes back.

## When the pool resets

Your pool resets at the start of each calendar month, not on your billing date. If you need more before then, you can upgrade at any time. Before you do, read [Beta & Grandfathered Pricing](/plans/beta-and-grandfathering), because changing tiers affects any locked-in rate.
