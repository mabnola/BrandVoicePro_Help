This walkthrough takes you from a blank account to your first branded article. You will need a piece of PLR content (or any generic draft) ready to paste in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Head to [app.brandvoiceproapp.com](https://app.brandvoiceproapp.com) and sign up with your email address and a password of at least eight characters. The free plan needs no credit card and gives you enough generations to see whether the tool solves your problem.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build a Brand Profile">
    In the sidebar, select **New Brand Profile** and fill in your details: profile name, company or brand name, your origin story, and the specific message you want highlighted. Paid plans unlock the rest of the profile, including brand voice, personality, niche, and target audience. See [Brand Profiles](/using/brand-profiles) for the full field-by-field guide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste your content">
    On the **Customize** tab, paste your PLR article or draft into the source box. A live counter shows your word count against your plan limit: 1,500 words on Free, 3,000 on Basic, 5,000 on Premium.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a rewrite level">
    Pick **light**, **moderate**, or **deep**, and optionally add focus areas such as Stronger Hook or Add CTAs. Moderate is the default and a good place to start.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate">
    Select **Generate**. The rewrite streams into the panel as it is written, and your profile shapes the voice, examples, and framing throughout. Five SEO title options appear underneath when the rewrite finishes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add social posts (optional)">
    Switch to the **Collateral** tab, paste your content, pick your platforms, and generate posts for all six platforms in one request. See [Social Posts](/using/social-posts).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy or download">
    Copy the result with its formatting intact, or save it as **.txt**, **.md**, **.rtf**, or **.docx**. Saving also files the piece in your History so you can reopen it later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Your first generation sets the tone. The more specific your origin story and highlight message, the more the output sounds like you and not like a polished template.
</Tip>

## What counts as one generation

One article personalization uses one generation. One social post request uses one generation, no matter how many platforms it covers. SEO title suggestions are free. Full details are on [Generations Explained](/plans/generations-explained).
