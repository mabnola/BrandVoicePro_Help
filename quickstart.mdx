This walkthrough takes you from a blank account to your first branded article. You will need a piece of PLR content (or any generic draft) ready to paste in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Head to [app.brandvoiceproapp.com](https://app.brandvoiceproapp.com) and sign up. The free plan needs no credit card and gives you enough generations to see whether the tool solves your problem.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build a Brand Profile">
    Answer a short set of questions about your brand: your name, your audience, your origin story, what you do, the message you want to highlight, and your goal for the content. This is your profile, and BrandVoice Pro uses it on every generation. See [Brand Profiles](/using/brand-profiles) for the full field-by-field guide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste your content">
    Drop in your PLR article or draft. BrandVoice Pro is built for standard article lengths, so a typical PLR piece fits comfortably. Word limits vary by plan; see [Plans & Pricing](/plans/plans-and-pricing).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate">
    BrandVoice Pro rewrites the piece using your profile. Your story opens the article, echoes through the middle, and ties the conclusion together. The result reads like you wrote it from experience.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add social posts (optional)">
    In the same workflow, generate platform-specific posts for all six major platforms at once. See [Social Posts](/using/social-posts).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review, copy, and publish">
    Read it through, make any tweaks, and publish it wherever your content lives. Your voice, your audience, done.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Your first generation sets the tone. The more specific your origin story and highlight message, the more the output sounds like you and not like a polished template.
</Tip>

## What counts as one generation

One article personalization uses one generation. One social post request uses one generation, no matter how many platforms it covers. Full details are on [Generations Explained](/plans/generations-explained).
