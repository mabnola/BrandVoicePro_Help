Personalization is the core of BrandVoice Pro. You bring a generic piece, your Brand Profile does the rest, and the output reads like you wrote it from personal experience.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick a profile">
    Choose the [Brand Profile](/using/brand-profiles) you want to write as. If you only have one, it is selected for you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste your content">
    Drop in your PLR article or draft. BrandVoice Pro is built for standard article lengths, which keeps the output tight and authentic rather than sprawling.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate">
    The app rewrites the piece using your profile and the SPEAK Method. Your origin story opens the article, relevant points from your highlight message echo through the middle, and the conclusion ties back to your brand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and refine">
    Read it through. If you want a different angle, adjust your profile or highlight message and generate again.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hand off to Step 2 (optional)">
    When the article looks good, click **Send to Step 2** on the result header to drop the finished text straight into Generate Collateral. No copy and paste — the article is seeded into the base-content box, ready to spin into platform-specific social posts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What makes it different from a rewrite

A generic AI rewrite changes words. BrandVoice Pro changes whose voice the piece is in. It does not just swap your name into a template thousands of others are publishing. It weaves your actual story and audience language throughout, so a reader feels like they know the person behind the brand.

<Note>
  Personalization quality is highest when the app can focus on a well-scoped piece. That is why BrandVoice Pro is tuned for standard article lengths rather than very long documents.
</Note>

## Works beyond PLR

BrandVoice Pro was built for the PLR personalization workflow, but it works just as well for rewriting your own drafts, refreshing older posts, or generating branded content from scratch. Any workflow where consistency and authenticity matter benefits from it.
