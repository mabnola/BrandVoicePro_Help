BrandVoice Pro generates social posts for all six major platforms in one go: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, Threads, and X. Each post is tailored to that platform's tone and format, not the same caption pasted six times.

## How to generate social posts

You can create social posts two ways:

- **From a personalized article (Step 1 → Step 2).** After you rewrite a piece in Customize (Step 1), use the **Send to Step 2** button on the result header to hand the finished article straight into Generate Collateral. No copy and paste — the rewritten text is seeded into the base-content box on Step 2, ready to generate posts from.
- **On their own.** Skip Step 1 and paste any content directly into Generate Collateral when you just need to fill your social calendar.

Either way, the posts use your Brand Profile, so they carry your voice and speak to your audience the same way your articles do.

## Content Extras apply to Step 1, not to social posts

Content Extras — TL;DR, FAQ, reflection questions, motivational quotes, personal-story hooks, relevant statistics, and discussion questions — shape the long-form article you generate in Step 1 (Customize). They are no longer applied to short social posts in Step 2, because those formats belong on a full article, not on a 280-character caption.

What still shapes your social posts in Step 2:

- **Structure preference.** Choose a layout (for example FAQ format or Comparison layout) to influence how each platform's post is framed.
- **Custom K.** Add your own one-off instruction for this generation — a specific angle, hook style, or emphasis.

Everything else — your voice, story, audience, and CTA — still flows from your Brand Profile.

## One request, one generation

A single social post request counts as one generation, no matter how many platforms it covers. Generating posts for all six platforms still uses one generation from your monthly pool. See [Generations Explained](/plans/generations-explained) for how the shared pool works.

<Tip>
  Because posts are tailored per platform, you can publish them as-is or use them as strong first drafts. A LinkedIn post reads like LinkedIn; a TikTok caption reads like TikTok.
</Tip>
